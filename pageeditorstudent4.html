Sitemap

Technical Description

HTML Structure and Functionality

SVG Structure:
The sitemap is created using SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics) to provide precise layout control and interactivity with links.

Navigation:
Each page in the sitemap is represented by a rectangular node, with links to their respective pages. Nodes like "Splash Screen," "Home," "Volunteer," "Table," "User Profile," "Feedback," "Team," and "Sitemap" are connected to create the flow of the website.

Content and Page Editor:
The "Content Pages" and "Page Editor" sections are divided into child nodes like "Content 01," "Content 02," "Student 01," "Student 02," etc., connected to represent their hierarchy.

Interactivity:
Nodes are clickable using <a> tags wrapped around <g> (group) elements in SVG.

Design Considerations:
Different colors are used to distinguish sections, and rounded corners are applied for a smooth appearance. The text is centered for readability.

Lines:
<line> elements are used to connect the nodes, visually representing the flow between pages.

Accessibility

Accessibility Features in the Sitemap

1. Semantic HTML:
Proper use of <svg>, <a>, and <line> elements enhances accessibility.

2. Text Readability & Contrast:
Adequate contrast and easy-to-read fonts are chosen for the text inside each node.

3. Alternative Text for Images:
Images or icons used for nodes will have alt text to ensure accessibility for screen readers.

4. Keyboard & Screen Reader Support:
The sitemap is designed to be navigable using a keyboard and compatible with screen readers.

5. Motion Sensitivity Considerations:
The sitemap avoids excessive animations that may cause discomfort for users with motion sensitivity.

Content Page 4

In this section, we explore the efforts to combat poaching and its effects on wildlife conservation.

Introduction

Poaching has a significant negative impact on ecosystems around the world. It threatens biodiversity and disrupts natural habitats.

Commonly Poached Species

  • Elephants: Targeted for their ivory tusks.
  • Rhinos: Poached for their horns.
  • Tigers: Hunted for their pelts and bones.

Anti-Poaching Measures

Efforts to combat poaching include:

  • Increased law enforcement patrols.
  • Community-based conservation programs.
  • International collaborations to stop illegal wildlife trade.

How You Can Help

Individuals can support anti-poaching efforts by:

  • Donating to wildlife conservation organizations.
  • Raising awareness about the dangers of poaching.
  • Avoiding products made from endangered species.
